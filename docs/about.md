### Risk Consultant I

**The Financial Risk Group** · Cary, NC · Jun 2016 – Dec 2020
{ .subtitle }

This period centered on a multi-year consolidation effort—unifying fragmented risk platforms into a single governed system. The starting point was typical of legacy environments: manually versioned codebases, Excel files, scattered databases, and upstream systems without SLAs. One project involved untangling a complex Excel workbook into a maintainable codebase on their internal platform. I developed the project plan, executed the translation, shepherded it through QA and validation, and supported the production release. The 4-hour-to-15-minute pipeline optimization came from partitioning and indexing, and reducing I/O by pushing processing back into the database where possible.

- Delivered CCAR, DFAST, and IFRS9 analytical systems supporting capital planning and regulatory compliance for large financial institutions
- Consolidated forecasting platforms, retiring fragmented legacy systems and centralizing reporting pipelines into maintainable architectures
- Optimized high-volume database ingestion pipeline, reducing runtime from **4 hours to 15 minutes** through partitioning, indexing, and parallelization
- Partnered with finance and risk stakeholders to define business requirements, translate regulatory logic, and validate model assumptions before production deployment

### Data Warehouse Solutions Architect

**Bank of America** · Charlotte, NC · Jan 2015 – Jun 2016
{ .subtitle }

The $17B LCR impact came from giving the bank accurate visibility into their FDIC requirements—prior to this project, they were significantly over-estimating. The technical challenge was data volume and lag logic: a single day's calculation required multiple days of account-level deposit history. The bottleneck was I/O, so I pushed processing back into the database and only pulled aggregated results when necessary.

- Designed FDIC calculation engine processing **163 million records daily**, reducing LCR outflow by **$17B** and resolving a Federal Reserve MRA
- Led ETL and data warehouse architecture for 40+ onshore and offshore developers; standardized development practices
- Devised technical solution for Bank of America's Federal Reserve 5G Liquidity report
- Redesigned Operational Deposits data warehouse feed, reducing runtime from **4 hours to 55 minutes**
